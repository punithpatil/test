About us

“Project Jatayu” is a student technical team of R.V College of Engineering, Bengaluru, India.

The team’s mission lies in the research and development of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAV’s) for real time deployment to aid and assist the work completion in swift and efficient manner.

The team founded in the early 2009 has successfully developed and tested various UAVs. The team actively takes part in internationally acclaimed events like Australian Outback challenge, SUAS and many other national level competitions.

Agasthya

The first ever prototype built way back in the year 2009. The prototype was spear headed by an all Electronics and Communications engineering students. Made of balsa wood, this mammoth model was able to give stiff competition to all other opponents in the Australian Outback challenge, finishing 3rd in the event which saw many corporate teams battling out for the title.

Vayu

The plane which carried out the missions in SUAS 2012 saw a shift and inclination towards betterment of EC subsystems than the Mechanical. A Courage airframe was used for this purpose. The team shifted from the previously used Paparazzi autopilot system to the Ardupilot system. The plane completed image recognition using its RMRC 480N, Sony CCD camera.

Sampathi

A commercially available Wilga model was modified to test and carry out package dropping mechanism with pin point accuracy. It was also used to perfect the “Net hack” where the plane could hack into the internet available below it to retrieve any image or document file.

Drona

The SUAS 2014 model saw the use of a modified Ranger EX model. For the first time a push type propeller system was used. This UAV is now successfully able to complete autonomous take off, navigation, landing along with package drop and image recognition of letter, color and shapes. It uses an Ardupilot 2.6 Mega and BOSCAM for this purpose.
